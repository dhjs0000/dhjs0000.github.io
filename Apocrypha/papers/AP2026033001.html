摘要

We present the Associative Motor Architecture (AMA), a conceptual framework for artificial intelligence that rejects monolithic function approximation in favor of six-layer neural federalism. Drawing from vertebrate neurobiology, AMA distributes cognitive sovereignty across distinct temporal scales: from millisecond-scale spinal reflexes (L0) capable of vetoing higher layers, to second-scale narrative consciousness (L5) operating via single-stream broadcasting.
